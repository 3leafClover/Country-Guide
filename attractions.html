Attractions

The Burj Khalifa is a mixed-use skyscraper located in Dubai, U.A.E. It is the world's tallest building, according to all three of the main criteria by which such buildings are judged. The Burj Khalifa was officially named to honour the president of the neighbouring emirate of Abu Dhabi, Sheikh Khalīfah ibn Zāyid Āl Nahyān.

Not only is Burj Khalifa the world's tallest building but it has also broken two other impressive records: tallest structure, previously held by the KVLY-TV mast in Blanchard, North Dakota, and tallest free-standing structure, previously held by Toronto's CN Tower.

The Burj Al Arab is a luxury hotel located in the city of Dubai, United Arab Emirates. Managed by Jumeirah hotel group, it is one of the tallest hotels in the world, although 39% of its total height is made up of non-occupiable space.

Facilities here include a spacious playroom, large plasma screen TV, bedroom with soft bed and baby cot, and nutritious private dining. Guests also have complimentary use of Sinbad's Kids' Club at nearby Jumeirah Beach Hotel, which has a range of outdoor activities.

Widely considered the best mall in Dubai, this shopping centre features several flagship stores and a dedicated fashion avenue boasting high-fashion brands including Louis Vuitton, Prada, Gucci and more. Other stores in The Dubai Mall include Galeries Lafayette, Bloomingdales and Pottery Barn.

A mall like no other, Dubai Mall has more than 1,200 retail outlets, including two anchor department stores, Galeries Lafayette and Bloomingdale's, along with over 200 international dining experiences and a wide range of entertainment and leisure attractions for families to enjoy.
